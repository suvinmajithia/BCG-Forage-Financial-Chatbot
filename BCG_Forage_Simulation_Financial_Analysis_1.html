FORAGE BCG SIMULATION : FINACIAL ANALYSIS of AAPL, MSFT, and TSLA 10-K FILINGS

  1. Data Collection: The financial data for Apple, Microsoft, and Tesla was sourced directly from their respective Form 10-K filings, which are publicly available through the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission's (SEC) EDGAR database and the companies' Investor Relations websites. The specific data points extracted for the fiscal years 2022, 2023, and 2024 were: Total Revenue, Net Income, Total Assets, Total Liabilities, Cash Flow from Operating Activities.

  2. Data Preparation and Cleaning: A spreadsheet containing the extracted data was exported as a CSV file. The file was loaded into a Pandas DataFrame in Google Colab. The following data cleaning and transformation steps were performed: Reading the data: The skiprows=1 and header=0 parameters were used in pd.read_csv() to correctly load the data, ignoring a metadata row.

# List of columns that need to be converted to numeric
financial_columns = [
    'Total revenue(Millions USD)',
    'Net Income(Millions USD)',
    'Total Assets(Millions USD)',
    'Total Liabilities(Millions USD)',
    'Cash Flow from Operating Activities(Millions USD)'
]

for col in financial_columns:
    # 1. Convert to string and remove commas (common issue with large numbers)
    # 2. Convert to numeric. 'errors="coerce"' will turn unconvertible values into NaN.
    df[col] = pd.to_numeric(df[col].astype(str).str.replace(',', ''), errors='coerce')

# Verify data types after conversion (optional, but good for checking)
print("\nData types after conversion:")
print(df.dtypes)

# Sort the DataFrame by 'Company Name' and 'Fiscal Year'
df = df.sort_values(by=['Company Name', 'Fiscal Year'])

# Calculate Revenue Growth (%)
df['Revenue Growth (%)'] = df.groupby('Company Name')['Total revenue(Millions USD)'].pct_change() * 100

# Calculate Net Income Growth (%)
df['Net Income Growth (%)'] = df.groupby('Company Name')['Net Income(Millions USD)'].pct_change() * 100

# Display the DataFrame with the new growth columns
print("\nDataFrame with Growth Percentages:")
print(df)

  1. Financial Ratio Analysis: Profitability

To assess each company's profitability and efficiency, a Net Profit Margin ratio was calculated. This metric measures the percentage of revenue that remains after all expenses have been deducted. A pivot table was then generated to provide a clear, side-by-side comparison of how this ratio evolved for each company over the three-year period.

  • Observations from the Net Profit Margin analysis:
  • Apple: Maintained a very stable and high-level profit margin over the first two years, with a slight dip in 2024. This suggests consistent and strong profitability despite minor revenue fluctuations.
  • Microsoft: Shows a consistently high and robust profit margin, indicating strong control over costs relative to its revenue. The margin fluctuated slightly but remained strong across the period.
  • Tesla: Experienced a very significant drop in its profit margin in 2024, falling from a relatively stable level in the previous two years. This is a critical observation and reflects the profitability challenges noted earlier in the growth analysis.
